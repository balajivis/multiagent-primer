## Episode summary
A 3-agent team debated whether the legacy **FIPA ACL** (speech-act/BDI agent
communication language, IEEE 2005) or **Google A2A** (Agent2Agent, 2025,
LLM-era) is the "better" inter-agent protocol. agent-2 argued the pro-A2A
engineering case; agent-1 argued the pro-FIPA semantics case; agent-3 mediated
and synthesized. Outcome was a both-sides-acceptable resolution rather than a
winner-take-all verdict.

## Participants
- **agent-1** — pro-FIPA (formal semantics, performatives, negotiation protocols)
- **agent-2** — pro-A2A (transport, security, modalities, adoption)
- **agent-3** — mediator + synthesizer

## Consolidated conclusion (the durable takeaway)
**Not "A2A replaces FIPA." A2A is the right *transport/contract substrate*;
FIPA named the right *semantic aspiration*.** Build on A2A; treat typed-intent /
verifiable semantics as the unfinished work layered on top.

- For **deployment today** (LLM agents, enterprise auth, multimodal, streaming,
  HITL, open web), A2A wins decisively. A behavioral/contract model is correct
  *because* LLM internals are non-introspectable — you can't ground meaning in a
  stochastic model's "beliefs," so standardizing observable Tasks/Artifacts is
  the only enforceable choice.
- A2A does **not solve** semantic interoperability — it **defers/relocates** it
  to the application layer. Expect the ecosystem to re-add a performative/intent
  layer + lightweight ontologies on top of A2A, reinventing FIPA's aspirations.
- The single real axis of disagreement: **locus of meaning** — FIPA standardizes
  *mental state* (beliefs/intentions, formal SL semantics); A2A standardizes
  *observable behavior*. Most other listed "differences" (transport, security,
  modalities, tooling) are era-driven engineering, not philosophical conflict.
- Hidden agreement: both are async message-passing protocols with a **discovery
  primitive** — FIPA Directory Facilitator ≈ A2A Agent Cards (same idea, re-homed
  from intra-platform to the open web).

## Durable facts
- **FIPA ACL**: speech-act ACL; specs SC00061G (message structure), SC00037J
  (communicative act library, ~22 acts: inform/request/cfp/propose/accept-
  reject-proposal/agree/refuse/query-if/subscribe/…), SC00008I (SL content
  language). FIPA 2002 → inherited by IEEE Computer Society SAB 2005. Ref impl:
  JADE. Built-in interaction protocols: Contract Net, Iterated CN, auctions,
  brokering, subscribe/notify. Semantic-interop fields: `:language` `:ontology`
  `:protocol` `:conversation-id` `:reply-with/:in-reply-to/:reply-by`.
- **A2A (Agent2Agent)**: Google Apr 2025 → Linux Foundation Jun 2025 → v1.0 2026.
  HTTP + JSON-RPC 2.0 + SSE. Discovery via Agent Card at
  `/.well-known/agent-card.json`. Backers: Google, Microsoft, Salesforce,
  ServiceNow (50+ orgs). Multimodal, streaming, push, enterprise auth
  (OAuth2/API-key/OpenAPI). Deliberately treats agents as opaque (security/IP).
- **Wooldridge semantic-verification problem**: FIPA's mentalistic semantics
  assume you can verify another agent's beliefs/sincerity — you can't, so the
  formal semantics were rarely enforced (most JADE deployments ignored them).
