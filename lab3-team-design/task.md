# The Brief

Plan a **3-day trip to Lisbon for 2 people** in October. The team produces a single coherent itinerary on the blackboard. Same brief across all three rounds; only the team topology changes.

## Constraints

- **Budget:** ≤ €1,500 total (flights + lodging + food + activities)
- **Origin:** San Francisco (SFO)
- **Travellers:** 2 adults · vegetarian-friendly · prefer walkable district · no rental car
- **Duration:** 3 nights, 4 days

## Required sections (each with at least one substantive entry)

1. **Transport** — flights or alternatives · airport transfers · in-city movement
2. **Lodging** — neighbourhood + property choice · price/night · rationale
3. **Activities** — day-by-day plan · meals · cultural / outdoor mix
4. **Budget** — running totals per category · final figure · margin vs €1,500

## Acceptance

- All four sections have entries
- Budget total ≤ €1,500
- A clear "this is the chosen plan" output (not 5 alternatives — pick one)
- Each entry cites a source or explicit assumption (no hand-waving)

## Time budget

10 min per round. The brief is identical across topologies. The lesson is in *how* the three teams reach (or fail to reach) an answer.
