# The Brief

Produce a **~400-word brief** on Stanford's *Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior* (Park et al., 2023) — the "Smallville" experiment.

## Required sections (each must have at least 2 findings on the blackboard before you synthesise)

1. **Setup** — How many agents? What environment? What architecture (memory, planning, reflection)? Which model?
2. **Emergent behaviours** — What did the agents do that the researchers did not script? Pick the two most striking examples and cite them.
3. **Limitations** — What broke, drifted, or required human intervention? Be specific — "hallucination" is not specific.
4. **Implications for production multi-agent systems in 2026** — What does this paper actually predict about systems being deployed today? Be skeptical: which findings have held up, and which haven't?

## Acceptance criteria

- Each section has at least two findings on the board, each with a source
- Final synthesis is ~400 words total
- Inline attribution to which agent contributed which finding
- No section is empty or padded

## Time budget

15 minutes. Do not optimise for completeness — optimise for *not duplicating each other's work*. A brief with 3 strong, distinct findings per section is better than 6 overlapping ones.
