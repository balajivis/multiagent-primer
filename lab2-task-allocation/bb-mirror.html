In Round 2 the CLI prevents races but not fairness. In Round 3, capabilities make allocation principled — and surface a new pain: orphan tasks nobody can do, and cheating when an agent claims outside its profile.

What to watch

A yellow-highlighted task means it's been claimed but not done in >5 min — possibly stuck. An orphan in Round 3 means no agent has the capability listed in needs.

Compare per-agent done counts. Big gaps = cherry-picking (R2) or imbalanced capabilities (R3).

The contract is the CLI

All claims go through ./task-cli/task (POSIX O_EXCL lock). The CLI doesn't enforce capability match — that's discipline, and discipline is the lesson.

Cheating shows up as hollow output, not a CLI error.
