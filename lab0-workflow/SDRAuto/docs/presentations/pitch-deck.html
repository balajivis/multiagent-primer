What It Does

Core loop: 5 stages

1
Source
Monitors job boards, funding signals, LinkedIn, and tech-stack data to find in-market accounts matching your ICP
2
Research
Builds a per-account brief: org chart signals, recent news, pain indicators, and a persona read on the buyer
3
Outreach
Writes and sends personalised first touches in the rep's cloned voice across email and LinkedIn, with timing intelligence
4
Reply
Classifies inbound replies: interest, objection, unsubscribe. Handles or escalates based on configured autonomy level
5
Book
Proposes calendar slots, handles rescheduling, logs the booked meeting to CRM with full conversation context

Key Design Decisions

The choices that define the product

Foundational
Human-in-the-loop design
Approve every message → approve first touch only → fully autonomous. The default must be opinionated.
Sender identity & deliverability
Real rep alias or branded persona? Domain warmup, SPF/DKIM, and daily caps designed in on day one.
Stateful memory across touches
Agent remembers what it sent, what was replied, and what was rejected — across all channels — without any rep logging.
Strategic
Lead sourcing vs. BYOL
Build-in lead finding (stronger moat, harder) vs. bring-your-own-list (faster to ship). Most start BYOL.
Personalisation depth vs. scale
Deep per-account research vs. high-volume templated sends are different products. Doing both dilutes both.
Channel scope
Email only is safest to start. Each additional channel multiplies state machine complexity significantly.

Pitfalls

Where autonomous BDRs fail

High Risk
Spam at scale
A misconfigured agent can burn a domain in days. Daily caps, bounce monitoring, and auto-pause must be bulletproof.
The "obviously AI" problem
Prospects increasingly detect AI-written email. Quality must clear a human-written bar.
Regulatory exposure
GDPR, CAN-SPAM, CCPA all constrain automated outreach. Operating across regions without consent management is a compliance time bomb.
Watch Out
Rep adoption failure
Reps who can't see what the agent is doing will route around it. A glass-box UI is non-negotiable.
Runaway reply handling
An "unsubscribe" reply that triggers a follow-up is a brand disaster. Reply classification is harder than it looks.
Attribution ambiguity
Without clear attribution, customers can't justify spend and you can't improve the product. Build it in from day one.

Autonomy Spectrum

Choose your control level

Approve everything
Agent drafts; human sends every message. Best for compliance-sensitive teams.
Low risk Low value
L2
First touch approval RECOMMENDED
Auto-follow-up; human approves only first contact. Best for most enterprise SDR teams.
Low-Med Med value
L3
Auto-send, review replies
All outreach automated; human handles replies only. Best for growth-stage scale-ups.
Med risk High value
L4
Fully autonomous
Agent handles sourcing, outreach, replies, booking end-to-end. Best for founders / early-stage.
High risk Highest
Recommended default: L2. Safe enough to launch, autonomous enough to save real time.

Recommended Build Path

Start narrow. Prove value fast. Expand with confidence.

Weeks 1–4
Email-only, BYOL, L2 autonomy. Use ~50 emails to clone rep style. Approve first touch, auto-follow-up. Prove reply rates before adding complexity.
Months 2–3
Add signal-based lead sourcing. Layer in intent signals (funding, hiring, job changes). Introduce real-time trigger outreach as a distinct flow.
Months 4–6
Expand channels + raise autonomy. Add LinkedIn. Move toward L3/L4 as trust is built. Ship the transparent reasoning log and self-improvement loop.
